How IND Wizard™ Works

Our revolutionary platform streamlines the entire IND submission process from initial data collection to final FDA submission with unprecedented efficiency.

1. Data Ingestion

Import source documents such as protocols, CSRs, and CMC data. The AI Harvester automatically extracts relevant information to pre-populate your IND sections.

Example: When uploading a clinical protocol PDF, the system automatically extracts study design, endpoints, inclusion/exclusion criteria, and dosing regimens, mapping them to the appropriate IND sections.

2. Collaborative Authoring

Multiple regulatory writers can work simultaneously on different sections. The system tracks changes, allows for inline comments, and manages version control automatically.

Example: Your CMC team works on Module 3 while your clinical team completes Module 2, with medical, regulatory, and QA reviewers providing real-time feedback using in-line comments.

3. Review & e-Signatures

Our CFR Part 11 compliant e-signature system enables reviewers and signers to review, approve, and sign submission sections with dual-authentication and a complete audit trail.

Example: Medical Director receives notification of sections ready for review, reviews content in the system, and applies their electronic signature, which is captured with a SHA-256 hash and stored in the immutable ledger.

4. PDF Generation & Validation

High-fidelity PDF generation with automatic pagination, table of contents, cross-references, and bookmarks. Built-in validators ensure compliance with FDA requirements.

Example: With one click, the system compiles the entire IND into a fully formatted PDF with proper pagination, headers/footers, and hyperlinked cross-references, then runs compliance checks before submission.

5. FDA Submission & Tracking

Direct submission to FDA ESG with package creation, automated acknowledgment tracking, and real-time status updates for all team members.

Example: Regulatory Ops uploads the final package directly to FDA ESG from within IND Wizard. The system then monitors for ACK1/2/3 acknowledgments and sends notifications as they are received.
